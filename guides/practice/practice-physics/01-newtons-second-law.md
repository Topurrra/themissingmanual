# Newton's second law

From [/guides/energy-forces-and-motion](/guides/energy-forces-and-motion):
`F = m * a` - the net force on an object equals its mass times its
acceleration. Rearranged, more mass needs more force for the same
acceleration; the formula is the receipt for the "shove an empty cart vs. a
full one" intuition.

The starter expression shows the shape with small numbers - swap in this
lesson's real ones.

**Your task:** a 1200 kg car needs to accelerate at 2.5 m/s². Using
`F = m * a`, what net force (in newtons) does that take?

**You'll practice:**

- Substituting real values into F = m * a
- Reading a physics formula as "multiply these two together," not algebra to solve
