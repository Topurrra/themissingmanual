# Momentum

From [/guides/energy-forces-and-motion](/guides/energy-forces-and-motion):
`p = m * v` - momentum is mass times velocity. A slow freight train and a
fast bullet can both have fearsome momentum: one from enormous mass, the
other from enormous speed. Momentum is conserved in a collision - the total
before equals the total after - which is what makes it useful for predicting
crash outcomes without simulating every millisecond.

The starter expression uses small numbers - swap in this lesson's real ones.

**Your task:** the same 1200 kg car from the force lesson is now moving at
18 m/s. Using `p = m * v`, what's its momentum, in kg·m/s?
