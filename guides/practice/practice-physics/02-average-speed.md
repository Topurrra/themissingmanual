# Average speed

From [/guides/what-physics-actually-is](/guides/what-physics-actually-is):
`speed = distance / time`. Units divide right alongside the numbers - km
divided by hours gives km/h, which is exactly what a speed should be measured
in. That's not a coincidence; it's your first free check that a formula is
built correctly.

The starter expression uses easy numbers - swap in this lesson's real ones.

**Your task:** a cyclist covers 150 km in 2.5 hours. Using
`speed = distance / time`, what's their average speed, in km/h?

**You'll practice:**

- Substituting real values into speed = distance / time
- Noticing that the units of your answer (km/h) confirm you divided, not multiplied
